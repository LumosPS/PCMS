# 01 — Brand Foundation

## What PCMS Is

PCMS (Palestinian Clinic Management System) is a multi-tenant healthcare SaaS that helps Palestinian clinics manage patients, appointments, visit records, invoices, and payments in Arabic and English.

## Positioning

One sentence, usable in marketing:

> PCMS gives Palestinian clinics a calm, reliable way to run their daily operations — in their language, with their patients' data kept safe.

Key differentiators to emphasize:

- Built for Palestinian clinics — Arabic-first, local workflows, ILS currency.
- Patient data safety as a foundation (tenant isolation, auditability), not a feature.
- Calm and simple where competing tools are cluttered.

## Audience

| Audience | What they care about | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Clinic owners / doctors | Trust, professionalism, patient privacy | Credible, precise, never gimmicky |
| Receptionists / staff | Speed, clarity, low training effort | Simple language, obvious actions |
| Marketing viewers (social) | Is this legitimate and relevant to me? | Consistent identity, local relevance |

Patients are **not** an audience for the product UI in MVP (patient login is out of scope), but marketing may speak about patient benefit.

## Personality: Calm & Clinical

The brand should feel like a well-run clinic: quiet, orderly, competent.

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Calm, reassuring | Loud, urgent, salesy |
| Precise, specific | Vague, exaggerated |
| Respectful, professional | Casual, slangy, jokey |
| Modern, clean | Trendy, decorated, flashy |
| Locally rooted | Generic "global startup" |

Every design and copy decision should pass the test: *would this feel at home in a trusted clinic?*

## Voice & Tone

### Principles (both languages)

- Short sentences. One idea each.
- Say what happened or what to do — no filler, no hype.
- Never alarm the user. Errors are stated calmly with a next step.
- Never blame the user ("you failed to…" is forbidden).
- No exclamation marks in product UI. Sparingly in marketing.

### Arabic

- Use Modern Standard Arabic (فصحى مبسطة) — professional but plain, no archaic constructions.
- Address the user with the formal singular.
- Do not transliterate English terms when a common Arabic term exists.
- Western digits (0–9) for all numbers, dates, amounts, and phone numbers.

### English

- Plain professional English. No idioms (many readers are ESL).
- Sentence case for headings and buttons, not Title Case.

### Examples

| Situation | English | Arabic |
|---|---|---|
| Success | Patient record saved. | تم حفظ بيانات المريض بنجاح. |
| Error | Could not save changes. Check the highlighted fields and try again. | تعذر حفظ التغييرات. يرجى مراجعة الحقول المحددة والمحاولة مرة أخرى. |
| Empty state | No appointments today. | لا توجد مواعيد اليوم. |
| Destructive confirm | This will cancel the appointment. The patient will not be notified automatically. | سيتم إلغاء الموعد. لن يتم إشعار المريض تلقائيًا. |
| Marketing headline | Run your clinic calmly. | أدِر عيادتك بهدوء. |

## Name Usage

- Product name: **PCMS** (all caps) in both languages; Arabic text may introduce it once as «نظام إدارة العيادات الفلسطينية (PCMS)» then use PCMS.
- Do not translate or inflect the name.
